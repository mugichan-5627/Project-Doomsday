Hackathon Submission — Full Description

> Project Doomsday is an institutional-grade financial intelligence engine that stress-tests company valuations against global Black Swan events using a multi-agent AI swarm, adversarial debate architecture, and contagion propagation modeling.
>
> How it works: Enter any stock ticker — US, Indian, or global. The system pulls live financial data via yfinance, resolves currencies to USD, and classifies the company by type (financial, high-growth, cyclical, mature, or loss-making). Then it unleashes a coordinated AI swarm:
>
> The Intelligence Layer executes 6 parallel search vectors across geopolitical, supply chain, financial, regulatory, technology, and macro domains using Tavily's real-time search API, synthesized by Google Gemini 2.0 Flash into exactly 6 specific, material risk scenarios — each tagged with geographic coordinates, revenue-at-risk percentages, probability estimates, and time horizons.
>
> The Adversarial Tribunal subjects every identified risk to a 3-agent debate. A Bear Advocate prosecutes the worst case with historical precedents and specific numbers. A Bull Advocate challenges the evidence, presenting mitigating factors and market pricing arguments. A Fracture Judge — operating at low temperature (0.3) for high-conviction decisions — delivers a final verdict with calibrated severity (1-10 scale: 8+ = catastrophic >25% impairment, 6-7 = material, <4 = dismissed entirely). Only risks that survive this tribunal enter the valuation model.
>
> The Global Fracture Map plots each validated risk as a geographic node on a dark-matter world map, with curved convergence lines arcing from each threat location toward company HQ — visualizing in real-time how geographically dispersed vulnerabilities converge on a single point of value. Node size and color pulse with severity. Hovering reveals multi-line intelligence briefs for each node.
>
> The Valuation Engine uses a 5-path context-aware routing system — because one formula cannot value every company honestly:
> - Path 1 (Financials): P/BV + Excess Return — because debt is a bank's product, not its liability, making DCF meaningless
> - Path 2 (High-Growth): EV/Revenue + Rule of 40 — because negative FCF makes terminal value >80% of a DCF, which is intellectually dishonest
> - Path 3 (Mature Profitable): 5-Year FCF-DCF + Gordon Growth Terminal — the only company type where traditional DCF is valid
> - Path 4 (Cyclicals): Normalized Mid-Cycle EBITDA with 0.8x haircut — because spot earnings at commodity peaks grossly overstate sustainable value
> - Path 5 (Loss-Making): EV/Revenue capped at 5x — no earnings means no discount, just an honest revenue anchor
>
> Base fair value is then anchored to market reality (capped at 1.2x current price for large caps, 1.35x mid caps, 1.5x small caps) because this is a stress-test tool, not a stock picker — we assume markets are broadly efficient and ask what breaks them.
>
> The Stress Waterfall applies four simultaneous destruction vectors — revenue haircuts, margin compression (in basis points), multiple de-rating, and WACC premium injection — each scaled by the chaos parameter and aggregate risk severity. The result renders as a Plotly waterfall chart showing step-by-step value destruction from base fair value down to distressed value, with the current market price marked as a reference line.
>
> The Contagion Cascade goes beyond isolated risk assessment. For the top 3 risks by severity, the engine models second, third, and fourth-order propagation effects — tracing how a single shock (e.g., Strait of Hormuz disruption) cascades through input costs → margin compression → credit rating downgrades → refinancing cost spikes → forced capex cuts. Each step quantifies the metric impacted, magnitude, time delay, and cumulative value destruction percentage. A compound contagion score models simultaneous chain crystallization with a crisis correlation estimate (rho approaching 1 as chaos increases) — because in real crises, diversification breaks down and everything correlates.
>
> The Transparency Layer — displayed at the bottom of every analysis — shows the complete audit trail: which valuation path was selected and why, raw financial inputs used, the exact formula with every number plugged in, how market anchoring was applied, stress decomposition showing each formula with chaos and severity substituted, and a final output table. Every calculation is reproducible. No black boxes.
>
> The Chaos Slider (0-100%) lets you sweep from mild macro headwinds to full systemic doomsday in real-time — instantly recomputing distressed valuations, stress factors, and downside percentages without re-running the 60-second AI pipeline. Slide from "MILD" through "MODERATE", "SEVERE", "CRISIS" to "DOOMSDAY" and watch value destruction accelerate non-linearly.
>
> Additional features:
> - Progressive terminal feed showing real-time swarm execution with timestamped logs
> - Automatic Indian ticker resolution (type RELIANCE, get RELIANCE.NS) with live INR/USD conversion
> - World state assessment (VIX, DXY, Oil, Gold, 10Y Yield) feeding into a Global Fear classification (CALM → CAUTIOUS → ANXIOUS → PANIC)
> - Investment Committee summary panel with top-5 ranked threats
> - Expandable debate transcripts showing full Bear/Bull/Judge reasoning for each risk
> - Dark military "command center" aesthetic with JetBrains Mono throughout, pulsing threat badges, and color-coded severity indicators
>
> Tech stack: Streamlit (frontend), Google Gemini 2.0 Flash (primary AI with NVIDIA/Fireworks failover), Tavily (real-time search), yfinance (market data), Plotly (maps, waterfall charts), custom CSS (dark monitor theme). Fully self-contained single-file deployment.
>
> The philosophy: Every institutional investor has a "what if everything goes wrong" scenario in their head. Project Doomsday makes that scenario quantitative, geographic, auditable, and interactive — in 60 seconds, for any company on Earth.
